What is a processor?

The CPU or processor is the brain of the computer. All information processing is done by the processor. The processor tells the computer what to do and when to do it. The speed of a CPU's information processing is measured in Megahertz or MHz. Processors also have cores, which are the processors way of multitasking. A CPU with 6 cores can process 6 different tasks at the same time. Hyperthreading is a technology that can benefit performance of multi-threaded applications that more efficiently schedules data manipulation from existing cores. A great, broken down explanation of hyperthreading can be found here

What kind of processor would my brain be?

The human brain is a very complicated organ. While the comparasin of the brain to a processor is not a perfect one, it is one of the best we have. Information processing of the brain starts with input from external sensory organs, which transform stimuli into electrochemical signals.

Recently, IBM has developed the most "brain-like" chip to date with 4,096 cores which is meant to mimic a million neurons and 256 million synapses of the brain. This processor was put to the test at Stanford University's Hoover Tower and the processor was able to recognize things such as pedestrians and vehicles with 80% accuracy.

What is a hard drive?

A hard disk drive, or HDD is a device used to permanently store and retrieve information. There are many variations, but their sizes are generally 3.5" and 2.5" for desktop and laptop computers respectively. A HDD consists of one or more discs to which data is written using a magnetic head, all inside of an air-sealed casing. HDD's Hard drives differ from SSD's (solid state drives) because they have mooving parts. Current HDD's can range from 250gb to even 10tb of storage. Even though SSD's can produce much faster read and write speeds, HDD's can have much higher capacities. More on that can be found here. If you were to buy a new laptop today it would have between 500gb and 1.5tb of internal storage. Some enthusiasts that build their own systems can have storage up to 15 terabytes of storage.

What kind of hard drive would my brain be?

If every brain has about 100 billion neurons, and each neuron is capable of making about 1,000 connections that represent 1,000 synapses, then it can be estimated that the average human brain could hold roughly 100 trillion packets of data. If each packet is a byte, that would be equal to 100 terabytes of data.

The computer metaphor somewhat degrades when coming to storage, because the difference between memory and storage must be deciphered. In computers it is simple, the hard drive is storage and the RAM is memory. When you shutdown the computer, the RAM gets erased but the HDD data is still stored. With the brain it is not that simple. One could say that school-learned things such as facts and general knowledge could be in "storage", and skills and functional knowledge such as skills or theories could be memory. This produces more issues though, because a singer's song lyrics could be both. Sure the singer has to sing the song and that could be classified as a skill, but many singers remember lyrics from songs they have not sang in years. The same is true with riding a bicycle. Since people don't seem to forget how to ride a bicycle, is that memory or storage?

What is RAM?

RAM or Random Access Memory is a the short-term memory of the computer. RAM comes in long thin sticks and plugs into the Motherboard of the system. RAM sizes are measured in Gigabytes or gb. RAM sizes start at 2gb and increase by factors of two with 4gb, 8gb, 16gb and so forth options being available.

Any program the computer uses has parts loaded into RAM while it is running. For example, if a user opens up Spotify and selects their favorite song, the computer will load some Spotify functionality as well as the song onto the RAM for access and playback. If too much RAM is used, in cases such as having too many Google Chrome tabs open, the computer will resort to storing data on the Hard Drive which can vastly slow down performance.

How much RAM does my brain have?

    To put it plainly, not much. The human brain can only store about 5 to 9 items in their short-term memory. This theory was created by George A. Miller in 1956 where he put volunteers through the digit-span test you can take here

    In this study, Miller concluded that humans can store about 5 to 9 "chunks" of data in the short term, which is about 15 to 30 seconds. The size of these chunks, however, the amount of information stored in each chunk can be of variable size.

     Say a volunteer is brought in and told a story that includes pictures about a man who drove to the store, bought some fruit, then drove home. If the volunteer is asked to recall everything they can about this story, they may remember the man, his car, and the fruit he bought. That would be 3 chunks of information. Within, say, the man's chunk, the volunteer may remember his appearance, shirt color, and name if one was given. This is an example of variable sizes of chunks of short-term memory data.

What is malware??

Malware is any software designed to damage or do unwanted actions on a computer system. Common examples of malware include Malware can come in many different variations such as Viruses, Trojan Horses, and Spyware.

Viruses are small programs or scripts (series of commands a computer follows like cooking directions) that can negatively affect the operations of a computer.Viruses have been known to create, move, and erase files, consume a computer's memory or RAM or cause a computer not to function correctly. Like real world viruses, they are known to duplicate themselves and spread to other systems -- commonly through infected emails.

Trojan Horses in computing are much like the are in Greek Mythology. A Trojan will apear to be an innocent software such as a game, utility, or antivirus program. Trojan horses can be extremely difficult to remove, because in cases the user had given the program privledges to change the computer as an administrator. Here is an interesting article of the 10 worst trojans of all time.

Spyware, as you can probably guess spys on your computer. Spyware can track virtually any activity on your computer from keypresses to web usage. Spyware is used a lot by advertisers to suggest ads to users based on their browsing history. Have you ever looked for an item on amazon and then seen ads for that item everywhere you went on the internet? Spyware did that. While this seems rather victimless, spyware can be used by individuals with more malicious intent to steal passwords, emails, and even credit card information.
